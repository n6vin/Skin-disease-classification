Description

Erythema multiforme is a skin reaction that can be triggered by an infection or some medicines. It's usually mild and goes away in a few weeks. There's also a rare, severe form that can affect the mouth, genitals and eyes and can be life-threatening.

Symptoms

Erythema often starts with sudden, red patches and blisters, usually on the palms of hands, feet, and face.Other symptoms are Flat, round red "targets", Itching, Cold sores, Fatigue, Joint pains, Fever.

Treatments

Avoiding harsh or perfumed soaps, detergents and lotions, as well as any known allergy triggers may help to reduce redness. Using an antihistamine or steroid cream may also help.
